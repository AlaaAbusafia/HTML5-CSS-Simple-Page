What’s in the White Paper?

Digital marketing is no longer about clicks or eyeballs. It’s about delivering actual business results. This white paper explains that performance-based marketing isn’t reliant on a single unit of measurement that can easily be gamed. Instead, it’s about how every element of advertising contributes to achieving a clearly de ned business result — as well as over laying path to conversion and ease of purchase.

The choice before modern marketers is simple: continue chasing ever-lower CPMs into the bottomless pit or shift to an approach based on quality and business results. Pure performance marketing is a choice you can make today.

Quantity over quality

Imagine if the performance of operators at a hypothetical call centre was measured only on the number of calls they made over the course of a day.

Before long they would be making as many calls as possible in a bid to keep superiors satis ed. While the number of calls will invariably increase, the quality of those calls will steadily decrease. As the workers chase ever-higher numbers, the attention that they pay to actually solving client problems will steadily decline as phone calls are rushed. In fact, it wouldn’t be far-fetched to imagine a particularly crafty operator that simply dials numbers and then puts the phone down as soon as someone answers. The numbers will certainly look great, but is this really what the business wants?

In digital advertising, we’ve seen something similar happen. The singular obsession with CPMs (cost per thousand views) has led to marketers and operators chase lower and lower costs.

Changing the rules

Acquire Online’s director Chris Schultz believes it’s time for marketers to introduce a few new rules to the game to ensure they’re getting the best bang for their buck.

He proposes a movement away from the CPM approach toward something that measures actual business performance. It’s about shifting the conversation from a single quantitative measure to something that considers the overall impact of an online campaign.

Pay as you go

A point Schultz points out, marketers will only pay for what is delivered. He compares it to law rms who agree to waive their service fee on the condition that they receive a portion of the settlement should they win a case.

“You’re only paying on what we’ve delivered,” says Schultz. “There’s a win- win for the client. Even though it might be capped at $3,000 for the month, but if we only deliver 10 at $10 each, then you’re only paying us $100.”

The creative gap

According to Global Web Index, the leading cause for adblock usage among New Zealanders is because they nd online ads annoying or irrelevant.

What this tells us is that there’s a major gap in creativity when it comes to this space. The ads being served at the moment are simply not interesting enough to deserve consumers’ time—and this is largely attributable to the way digital advertising is currently used.

Schultz says “moment marketing with the right message is particularly relevant when it comes re-marketing because consumers have a pretty low tolerance for seeing the same message over and over again.”
